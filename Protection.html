Protection

If you wish to protect yourself from infection and to slow transmission of COVID-19, do the following:

  • Wash your hands regularly with soap and water, or clean them with alcohol-based hand rub.

  • Maintain at least 1 metre distance between you and people coughing or sneezing

  • Avoid touching your face.

  • Practice physical distancing by avoiding unnecessary travel and staying away from large groups of people.

  • Cover your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing.(You should be doing this already!)

  • Refrain from smoking and other activities that weaken the lungs.

  • Stay home if you feel unwell.(Unless you need to go to a doctor.)

What is Self-Isolation:

Self-isolation is when you do not leave your home because you have or might have coronavirus (COVID-19).

How long should one self-isolate:

  • If you have symptoms or have tested positive you should self-isolate for at least 10 days

  • If someone you live with has symptoms or tested positive you should self-isolate for 14 days. The days start when the person in question first started having symptoms, if they did not show symptoms the 14 days start the day they were tested.
